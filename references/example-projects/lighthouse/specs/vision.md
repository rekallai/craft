# Vision — Lighthouse

## One-liner

Lighthouse helps small-business owners get paid faster by turning invoicing from a chore into a system that runs itself.

## The problem

Small businesses lose an average of 14 hours a month to invoicing administration — sending invoices, chasing late payments, reconciling deposits. The owners we've talked to don't want better invoice templates. They want the work to stop existing.

Existing tools (QuickBooks, FreshBooks, Wave) treat invoicing as a feature of accounting. That framing forces the user into a bookkeeper's mental model — chart of accounts, journal entries, reconciliations — when all they wanted was to send a bill and get paid.

## The bet

The next wave of small-business software won't be "easier accounting." It'll be **automation that respects the owner's time** — software that sends, follows up, and reconciles on its own, surfacing only the decisions a human actually needs to make.

Lighthouse starts narrow: send invoices, chase payment, deposit funds. No GL, no chart of accounts, no double-entry. We earn the right to expand by being the best in the world at the one thing owners hate most.

## Why now

- Payment rails (ACH, instant settlement, embedded finance) are finally cheap and programmable.
- LLMs make natural-language reconciliation tractable — "this $1,847 deposit is for invoice #204" no longer requires brittle rules.
- Small businesses have caught up culturally to subscription SaaS and instant onboarding.

## What we're not

- Not an accounting platform.
- Not a CRM.
- Not a "platform" with marketplace ambitions.

We are a tool that gets one job done so well the user forgets it exists.

## North star

**Hours saved per month, per active business.** Reported in the product, surfaced in marketing, used internally to prioritize.
